Environmental – Property Redevelopment (IV.7.)

Historic and cultural research plays a major part in this stage of development.

  • Since the community already has a preconceived idea of the use for that site, shifting their expectations is critical for acceptance.
  • Although a site may not have had a major impact on the community at large, it may have great importance to a few members personally.
  • It is important to engage in those conversations and understand how your development may incorporate their input.
  • Again, this will help gain acceptance into the community and may help to expedite municipal approvals.
  • On that same note, understanding existing historic designations and zoning restrictions at the beginning of the stage will help to form a redevelopment plan that meets the municipalities’ expectations and requirements for a faster approval process.
  • That faster approval process can also help mitigate costs of marketing strategies, design revisions, and lawyer fees.

Previous uses affect future development, positively or negatively must be identified.

Previously constructed improvement affect future development, positively or negatively must be identified.
