Environmental – Land Development (IV.3.)

Determine what environmental conditions must be met to begin land development.

  • Sediment and erosion fences
  • Dust and noise pollution
  • Wetlands and tree protection designation
  •  Protected natural areas (“do not disturb”)

Determine what regulated substances require special handling.

  • If a soil sample has a concentration of a certain element that is over a threshold level then it requires special handling.
  •  “Contaminated” is not a defined or a usable term

Determine plan to deal with toxic substances if encountered.

  • A pre-approved plan or protocol do deal with regulated substances will be invaluable once construction begins.
  •  A pre-approved plan will avoid work stoppages when regulated substances are encountered.
    • Soil removal plan
    • Water contamination plan
