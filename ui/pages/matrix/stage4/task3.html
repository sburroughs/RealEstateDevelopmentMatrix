III.4 Building Development Market Analysis and Strategies


Market Studies & Marketing Strategy – Building Development (III.4.)

Identify the most likely buyers or renters of the improved property.

  • Early identification of the prospects and a target marketing strategy is imperative.

Determine an attractive pricing policy.

  • Rental rates, lease terms, and tenant finish allowances must be competitive and reflective of the quality of the new space.

Determine the occupancy date for the newly constructed building.

  • The available occupancy date must be reasonable.
  • Is early occupancy available, if so, in what areas of the building?

Evaluate the financial opportunities for building operators.

  • Are the financial markets supportive of building operators acquiring a new building?
  • Are there financial institutions or pension funds that would like to finance he building?
  • Are there equity investors who would like to invest in the building?
  • Can the Building developer help the Builidng operator locate the capital to acquire the building?

Determine the lease-up period.

  • How long will it take to lease the entire building?
  • Are there incentives or inducements to accelerate the lease-up

Estimate the sales period to sell the newly constructed building.

  • The building developer begins marketing the finished building as soon as he acquires the site.
  • Sales interest in directly related to the leasing; more leasing begets more sales interest.

Establish a relationship with outside brokers and leasing agents to promote the building.

  • Outside brokers or leasing agents may call themselves “buyer agents’ or “tenant representatives”, but they should be very good friend with the Building Developer.
  • These people should be kept informed of the building’s progress and leasing status.
  • The Building Developer should have a vey clear compensation arrangement for these “outside agents.” They are your friends!
