Market Analysis and Strategies


he Marketing Tasks require the developer to determine what are the current and future market conditions for the expected end-uses(ers) of the property. The developer then must determine the most appropriate marketing strategy given the current and future market conditions. In implementing the marketing strategy, the developer must determine the appropriate marketing mix. The marketing tasks can be broken down into three categories: projected economic conditions, market strategy, and advertising and promotion (the marketing mix).

Projected Market Conditions: 

  • Determine what land uses should be developed on the site.
    •  Highest and Best Use.Graaskamp says this should be the most “acceptable use.”
    •  Future Market Demand
    • Future Competitive Supply
  • Determine when the land uses should be brought to market (absorption).
    • Construction time
    • Absorption time
  • Determine how and by whom the land uses should be brought to market.
    • In-house marketing and sales
    • Out-of-house marketing and sales (brokers, auctions, etc.)
    • In house & out-of-house combination

Market Strategies: 

  • What amount of various land uses should be developed on the site?
  • How and by whom should they be brought to market?
  • The best ways to determine the current and future market conditions is to:
    • Read, Read, Read (everything that you can find):
      • National & regional publications (Newspapers, magazines)
      • Local newsletters (on-line discussions of the regional and local economy
      • Online market surveys are produced by:
        • National and Regional Brokerage Firms
        • National and Regional market research firms
          • CoStar
          • Xcligent
          • Loopnet
    • Personally Network with local human beings such as:
      • Brokers
      • Business leaders
      • Politicians and government officials

Promotion and Advertising: 

  • Determine to whom the development be promoted and advertised.
    • Who is the target buyer/renter?
    • What is the best way to approach the target audience? 
  • Determine the sales pitch and who should make it.
    • Need a consistent theme.
    • Easily understood and presented. 
  • General property promotion to the community and neighborhood. 
  • Specific property promotion to motivated and capable buyers/renters. 
  • Determine the appropriate marketing mix (events, promotional materials, electronic advertising, site visits, etc.)
