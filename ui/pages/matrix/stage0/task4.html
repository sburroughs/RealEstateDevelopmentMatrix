Environmental


Phase I, II, and III Studies.

  • Phase I – Survey of site conditions; review existing documents and inspection of the property
  • Phase II – Testing of environmental site conditions. Determine existence of any regulated substances or suspicious conditions
  • Phase III- Remediation of certain environmental conditions or objectionable substances. 
  • After Phase III a “No further action letter” is the goal.

Certifications: 

  • U.S. Green Building Council – Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design (LEED)
  • Green Globes
  • Energy Star
  • Building Owners and Managers Association - BOMA360
  • Local/Regional Ordinances

Surface conditions to consider: wetlands, rivers, abandoned mines, grave yards, geological formations.

  • Atmospheric examples:
    • Sunlight, heat
    • Wind
    • Air pollution
  • Surface examples:
    • Flood Plains
    • Wetlands
    • Forestation
  • Subsurface examples:
    •  Water level or water table
    •  Soil Compaction
    •  Geological Strata 

Historic and Cultural: research title and survey history.

  • Are there historic events, cultural traditions, or religious considerations that can affect the development of the site?
  • Some resources for this research include:
    • Local historians
    • Anthropologists
    • Neighborhood activist groups 
    • Property archives and records
