Sales and Disposition


The Sales and Disposition Tasks require the developer to complete the seller’s due diligence, to market the property for sale, to negotiate and execute a sales contract and finally to negotiate any necessary development agreements. The developer may decide not to sell the property at this stage and continue on to the next stage of development. In this case, the disposition exercise is then blended in to the acquisition stage for the subsequent stage of development.

Seller’s Due Diligence:

  • What do we have?
    • Physical description
    •  Legal description
    • Economic description 
  • What is it worth?
    • Direct sales approach
    •  Replacement cost
    • Income approach 
  • Who wants to buy it?
    • Investors
    • Users
    • Other developers 
  • How do we effect a sale?
    • Purchase Price
    • Agreement terms & conditions 

Marketing:

  • Determine how the property should be presented to potential, qualified buyers:
    • Targeted versus broad/spray marketing
    • Internal/external brokers.
    •  Timeline
  • Develop an appropriate pricing strategy.
  •  Consider seller financing possibilities:
    •  Purchase options
    • Purchase-money mortgage
    • Land contract
  • Determine what risks, if any, the selling developer will retain.
  • Determine how competitive buyers will be treated. 

Sales Contract:

  • Price and terms
    • Representations and Warranties
  • Conditions to close
    • Buyer's due diligence period
    • Good faith deposit
    • Document preparation and review
    • Title and survey resolution
    • Public approvals
    • Timing
    • Tax Deferred Exchange requirements
  • Post-closing conditions 

Development Agreements: 

  • Development agreements allow land developer to sell almost finished land bays as finished land bay prices because of the binding Development Agreement that are executed at closing. These agreement protect the building developers because they address the following:
    • What must occur? (construction specifications)
    • When must they occur? (milestones)
    • What happens when they don’t occur? (guarantees, self-help, bonds)
    • Multiple buyers predicament

Closing: 

  • When and where does closing occur?
  • When does title pass?
    • Title from buyer to seller
    • When does Rent become the property of the buyer?
    • When does Insurance take effect for the buyer?
    • When does Liability transfer from seller to buyer?
    • When do Obligations transfer?
  • When and how are cash funds transferred?
    • Capital to Buyer
    • Buyer to Seller
    • Seller to Investors/Lenders
  • Who is notified and when?
    • Tenants
    • IRS
    • Municipality/Utilities
