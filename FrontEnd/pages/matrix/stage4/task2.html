II.4 Building Development Financing


Financing – Building Development (II.4.)

Like land development, this is a “beat the clock” game.

  • The building developer must be ready to start construction immediately so the that the improvements can be constructed and leased before the end of the construction loan.
  • Some times building developers arrange a “mini-perm” with the construction lender which provides for an automatic extension of the construction loan if needed.
  • Alternatively, the building developer can negotiate a “take-out” with another lender who will guarantee to pay off the construction lender (for a fee of course). The Building developer, the construction lender , and the take-out lender enter into a tri-party agreement to document this understanding.

Building Pad acquisition can require equity and some debt.

  • Equity capital is usually used for the acquisition of the building pad. The building pad becomes part of the collateral for the construction mortgage.
  • If debt capital is used to acquire the building pad, then this loan must be subordinated to the construction loan so that the construction loan will have a first lien position.

Hard costs are usually financed with an accruing construction loan.

  • The interest on the construction loan usually accrues during the construction and lease-up period so the developer does not need to make out-of-pockets payment to the lender.
  • However, this means that the developer must pay interest on interest. This is called compound interest and it accelerates the increase in the outstanding loan balance.

Soft costs are usually financed with equity contributions.

  • Most soft costs are not funded with the construction loan.
  • Therefore equity contribution must cover these important expenditures.

Pre-leases create value.

  • Who ever leases the building lease reduces the risk of the investment therefore increase the value of the projected.
  • A building that is 100% prelease to a credible tenant removes most of the market risk from the building developer.
  • Similarly, building that have significant pre-leasing, say 30-70% are considered mush less risky that buildings that are started with out any leasing (‘all Spec”).
  • Consequently, construction lenders will offer lower interest rates to preleased building development and reduce the level of person guarantees and credit enhancements.

Building can be sold prior to completion, which would require hold backs.

  • These situations have pre-sales agreements that provide for a purchase price that is funded in stages. 
  • Initial funding occurs at a particular level of construction.
  • Subsequent funding that are based on leasing activities.
    • Tenant improvements holdback for unleased spaces are funded when the lease for the space are executed so the improvements can be built.
    • Leasing commissions holdbacks for unleased space are funded when new leases are signed and the tenant occupies the space.
    • Economic earn-outs are usually passed on the level of rental income increase as value is created during the lease-up period.

Build-to-suit opportunities.

  • Space users who want to own and occupy their newly constructed building enter in to build-to-suit agreements with the building developer.
  • These may be special purpose-buildings like medical laboratories or manufacturing plants.
  • These be headquarter building with special, expensive finishes and meeting facilities.
  • The building developer’s risk is much lower in these situation so the sales price can be much lower, all other things being equal.
