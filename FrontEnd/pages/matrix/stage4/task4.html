Environmental – Building Development (IV.4.)

Building construction systems must meet “best practices”for approvals and certifications.

  • Most important is documentation and organization of the process (materials receipts, recycling reports, etc.).
  • Working with a reputable construction company familiar with sustainability will help to ensure that best practices are met and that any possible added costs of sustainability initiatives are minimized.
  • In previous years, the extra cost of environmental certifications was estimated at 3-5%; however, due to the broad acceptance of the practices, there is usually no extra costs associated, only extra paperwork.

Local ordinances are rapidly changing to create new sustainability benchmarks.

  • Some municipalities have begun requiring third-party certifications for new development.
  • Be aware of the current codes and of pending changes.

    • For example, Washington DC imposed the Green Code, which has higher standard than LEED, does not allow for majority glass buildings, and added greater expectations for storm water management.

Vest your approvals as soon as possible to avoid designs that may be affected by changes in environmental regulations.

  • With the ordinances changing so frequently, it is important to vest your approvals under current ordinances as quickly as possible to keep construction on schedule, on budget, and on design.
