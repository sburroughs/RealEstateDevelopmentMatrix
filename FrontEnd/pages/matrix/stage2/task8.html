VIII.2 Land Packaging Sales and Disposition


Disposition – Land Packaging (VIII.2.)

Determine the critical approvals required before the land developer can begin.

•Site plan approval

•Approval of plans and specification for land improvements

•Environmental approvals:

    • Soil management plan
    • Remediation plan approval
    • Water management plan for wetland preservation or modification

Determine if the land development team fulfill the contract conditions as well as meet the governmental regulations and requirements.

•Raise the required equity capital.

•Secure the necessary loans.

•Post the required bonds.

•Construct the improvement according to the approved plans and specifications.

•Assemble a credible and experienced land development team.

Evaluate seller financing opportunities.

•If a purchase-money mortgage is given by the seller, then it must be subordinated to the construction loan.

•Limited seller-financing opportunities beyond the purchase-money mortgage.

What if the land developer falls through (backs out, bankruptcy)?

•Is there a back door take-back?

    • Pre-arranged deed-in lieu of foreclosure agreement
    • Approved plans and specifications become the property of the seller.

•Land packager's liability:

    • Environmental liability
    • Law suits
    • Bond proceeds
