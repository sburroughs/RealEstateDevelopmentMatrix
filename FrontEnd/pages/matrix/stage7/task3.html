III.7 Property Redevelopment Market Studies and Marketing Strategies


Market Studies & Marketing Strategy – Property Redevelopment (III.7.)

The Redevelopment Market Tasks are very similar to the Land Bankers Market Tasks.

The land banker notes are at the bottom of these notes.

Determine if the long term trends and future market conditions support the redevelopment of the property.

  • Are there long term trends that would improve the economic prospects of this site by changing the community’s perception of the property
  • What are the alternative uses of the site? Adaptive Re-use potential.
    • Residential condominiums
    • Urban farming
    • Special events staging
    • Health care facilities
    • Mixed use developments

Determine if the existing property be modified or de-commissioned to the prepare for the future market opportunities.

  • Is there something that can be done now that would improve the adaptive re-use of the site?
    • Remove the railroad tracks
    • Remove dilapidated buildings
    • Remove special features like towers, kilns, vaults (unless they are historic)
    • Contaminated materials above ground

Identify interim land uses that be accommodated on the property.

  • That is, are there tenants in the market that could be accommodated on the existing property without impairing the future redevelopment of the property?

Determine the future off-site infrastructure developments that could enhance the possibility of re-development or “adaptive re-use.”

  • Future rail spur for industrial properties
  • Future transit station
  • Water and sewer availability
  • Future sports facility nearby

These are the Land Banking notes which may be helpful to the instructor:

The discussion of future trends should begin with the Charles Kettering quote,” We should al be concerned about the future because we will spend the rest of our lives there.” (Charles Kettering, 1876-1958, inventor, engineer, and businessman)

Land banker must identify general and specific long term trends and determine how they will effect the attractiveness (demand for) a specific property.

  • Identifying long-term trends is one thing, but interpreting those trends in terms of urban development and land use is much for difficult.
  • Charles Kettering is also credited with the saying, “The harder I work the luckier I get.”

The land banker must read widely and voraciously looking for new ideas and insights into future real estate development opportunities.

What kind of trends are we talking about?

  • Demographic change
  • Online shopping, education, commerce
  • Robotics
  • Health care and prevention
  • Globalization
  • Terrorism
  • Climate Change
  • Regulatory Environment
  • Infrastructure Improvements
  • Other trends?
    • Recycling
    • Immigration
    • Renewable Energy technologies
    • Transportation technologies

Determine how will the general community and political establishment will react to those trends.

  • Maintaining the status quo is a very strong force in communities.
  • Economic change will cause new financial winner and losers. Who are they?

When will the trends and the predicted effects occur?

  • Change will occur, but when?
    • During the land bankers holding period
    • Some land bankers have a very long holding period, some don’t.

Land Banker may focus on a particular event.

  • Completion of a transit way and/or a transit station
  • Completion of the Panama Canal widening and deepening
