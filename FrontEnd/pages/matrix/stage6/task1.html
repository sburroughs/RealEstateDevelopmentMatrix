I.6 Building Renovation Acquisition


Acquisitions – Building Renovations (I.6.)

Building Renovator are the most savvy and creative market analysts in the real estate development process:

  • They see trends developing before others.
  • They know and understand the special needs of certain kinds of tenants.
  • They identify and pre-lease to tenants who are ready to move to better or different space.

Evaluate the current market conditions for pricing anomalies or niche opportunities.

  • Determine if the rent differentials between various submarkets are justified in light of changing market conditions.
  • Are certain users being ignored such as start-up companies, technology companies, or companies with special occupancy issues such a security or communications.

Determine if the subject property be acquired, renovated, and repositioned to meet the market opportunities.

  • The subject property are often overlooked properties in quirky locations.
  • The current owners may not be aware of market opportunities.
  • The anticipated renovation improvements must be defined and accurately priced.
  • The market perception of the property must be changed during the renovation period. 

Determine if the renovation can be completed in a timely manner.

  • A ready and available renovator contractor is a must.
  • Government approvals and permits must be forthcoming.
  • Neighborhood issues must be readily resolvable. 

Develop a strategy to deal with holdover tenants.

  • Identify existing tenants who will remain in occupancy during the renovation period.
  • Determine if their business needs as well as health and safety can be met and insure during the renovation period.
  • Evaluate how the holdover tenants will fit in the marketing and leasing of the renovated building.
  • Can the holdover tenants be moved to another location during the renovation period? 

Determine if the “spread” is large enough to justify the risks incurred.

  • The building renovation is very risky.
  • May be ahead of the market.
  • May discover unexpected environmental, cultural, or historical issues.
  • May have difficulty integrating new building construction and building systems into the existing building.
  • May have unexpected issues with holdover tenants.
  • Do the expected profits justify incurring these risks?

Convince lenders and equity investors to support the planned renovations.

  • Many times local lenders and equity investors do not share the vision of the Building Renovator.
  • The best way to convince these skeptics of the validity of the new vision is with a pre-lease or a pre-sale.
  • Of course, a successful track record in building renovations is the best way to win support and attract the necessary capital.
