II.6 Building Renovation Financing


Financing – Building Renovations (II.6.)

Acquisition requires purchase of both land and building so debt and equity is usually required on the front end.

  • Debt capital for acquisition will usually have a first lien priority.
  • The acquisition loan will need to be subordinated to the construction loan.

Renovation loan (or construction loan) is usually an accrual loan that is tied to actual renovation construction (hard costs).

  • This is similar to the building construction loan.

Soft costs are usually covered by equity contribution.

  • This arrangement is similar to the building construction loan.

Holdover tenants must be taken into consideration and dealt with throughout the renovation period.

  • Holdover tenants may require special accommodations during the renovation period, such as entrances, security, or parking arrangements.
  • These additional costs need to funded either through the renovation loan or through equity reserves.

Like building development and land development, this is another “beat the clock” game; timing is critical.

  • The building renovator needs to be ready to start renovation as soon as the property is acquired.
  • If the renovation construction and re-leasing are not completed prior to the renovation loan term, the renovation loan will need to be extended or the property will be need to be refinanced.
