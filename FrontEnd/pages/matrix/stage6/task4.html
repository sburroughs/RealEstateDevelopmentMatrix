In building renovations, it is important to remember that you are dealing with previous mistakes, errors, and obsolete materials and technologies.

Asbestos, mold, and lead paint are some of the most common environmental issues during renovation.

  • It is important to have a plan in place for if these items are discovered and how to remediate them.
    • These issues are major considerations for not just the end users after renovation, but really for the workers performing the improvements, especially since asbestos and mold can be airborne issues.
    • There are professionals that specialize in remediation of toxic waste and materials to help create identification and remediation plans and to perform the remediation if needed.

Ensure code compliance for all updated codes, once a renovation starts you are no longer grandfathered in.

Enhance positive historical and cultural aspects of the renovation.

Proper recycling of disposed materials is critical.

  • Use off-site sorting of recycle materials if possible

Certifications are a nice touch to demonstrate the significant value of the renovation.
