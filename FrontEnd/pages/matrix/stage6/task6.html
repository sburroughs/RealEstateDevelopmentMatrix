Improvements – Building Renovations (VI.6.)

Design, engineer, and construct major building renovations.

  • Design-Bid-Build vs Design-Build
  • Unexpected conditions may be discovered:
    • Rotten wood
    • Mold conditions
    • Asbestos insulation
  • Construction management contract based on time and materials may be appropriate.
  • Experienced renovation contractors are critical.

Design drawings and renderings are critical to help stakeholders embrace the proposed improvements.

  • Two dimensional drawings are not “reader friendly."
  • Models, photographs, renderings, and videos can tell the story much better.
  • Video technology is advancing so rapidly that virtual tours are now relatively inexpensive.

Engineering drawings and renovation contractors must integrate new systems into the existing systems and public infrastructure.

  • Existing building systems must be evaluated to determine if they should be saved or replaced new.
  • Public infrastructure tie-in is more problematic:
      • Existing storm water systems are hard to expand.
      • Existing electric power systems are more adaptable.
      • Communication systems are constantly changing.

Renovation contractors must build new improvements while preserving old improvements and features.

  • Façade preservation or restoration is a public event!
  • Document all work, before and after.
  • Reach agreement with historical societies and preservation board prior to beginning construction.
  • Trees have feelings too!
      • Historic trees must be preserved or at least have a plan for restoration.
      • Historic landscaping is hard to define, but vigorously defended.
