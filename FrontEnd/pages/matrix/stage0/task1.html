Acquisition


Each stage of development begins with the Acquisition Tasks. Often the Acquisition Tasks are broken down into five categories: feasibility studies, underwriting requirements, contract negotiations, and closing conditions.

 

  • Feasibility:  Do the anticipated future benefits exceed the expected future costs?
    • Physical and engineering feasibility:
      • Properties physical characteristics
      • Cost to build whatever?
    • Market feasibility:
      • Current market conditions 
      • Future market conditions
    • Financial feasibility:
      • Simple Measures of Profitability
      • Complex measures of Profitability
    • Do the anticipated future benefits exceed the expected future costs? 
  • Underwriting:  Can capital be attracted to this development given the risk and returns of the development?
    • Required Rates of Returns
    •  How much capital?
    •  Who has this capital?
    • How long is the capital needed?
  • Contract:   How is title, control, monies, and associated risks transferred from the seller to the buyer?
    • The purchase contract defines how the title, control, monies, and associated risks are transferred from the seller to the buyer.
      • What rights are being transferred?
      • What representations and warranties are being made?
      • To whom; from whom?
      • Conditions to close? (Pre-closing conditions)
      • Deadlines, milestones, deposits?
      • Post-closing conditions? 
  • Due Diligence:  Are the assumptions about development (legal, physical, economic, market) valid or have they been verified?
    • Confirm that the assumptions about future development (legal, physical, financial, market) are valid and have been verified?
      • Multi-disciplinary exercise
      • No “limiting assumptions” –No excuses
    • •Confirm that there is a reasonable possibility that this acquisition will meet or exceed the buyer’s development expectations and investment objectives.
    • Ideally, the due diligence is performed before the good faith deposit become non-refundable 
  • Closing:  Have all of the required conditions to close been met, and have all of the required documents been prepared, reviewed, and executed?
    • All of the required conditions to close must be met.
    •  All of the required documents have been prepared, reviewed, and executed.
    • The capital is ready to be transferred.
    • The post-closing obligations (development agreements) have been defined, documented, and guaranteed
