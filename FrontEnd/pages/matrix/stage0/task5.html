Approvals and Permits


In every stage that there are the required approvals and permits needed to operate , improve, or modify the property during the development. The developer must not only determine what approvals and permits are necessary, but he must also acquire these approval and permits in a timely and cost effective manner. The approvals may be at the federal, state or municipal level and they also may be from regional authorities or private individuals or organizations.

Developers must realize and accept the natural tension that occurs during this process.

  • Natural Tension Occurs:
    •  Between private and public ownership rights.
    •  Between federal, state, regional, and local governments.
    •  Between various departments and commissions within a government.
    • Between adjacent land uses (neighbors).
  •  Every stage of the real estate development process requires some kind of governmental, quasi-governmental, or private approvals and/or permits. 
  •  Remember: Real estate is a public good

Developers must communicate with and understand the various individuals and agencies involved.

  • Don’t fight, make friends.
    •  No one is going away.
  •  Find areas of agreement.
    •  Understand your needs and the needs of others.
  • Structure workable compromises.
    • Win-win solutions are imperative.
  • Find timely resolutions.
    • Time wounds all deals.
    • Einstein once said, "The most powerful force in the world is compound interest."

Developers must expect, and prepare for:

  • The worst:
    • delays
    • loss of confidentiality
    • solicitation.
  • To have prior agreements or understanding retracted or rebuked..
  • To have confidential information made public.
  • To address social and community problems that the developer cannot solve.
  • To appreciate a variety of points of view that may at first seem illogical or irrelevant.
  • To appreciate the perceived realities of various stakeholders.
  • To reject illegal, inappropriate, and questionable offers in the name of “finding a solution.”

Developers must evaluate approvals and permits required at all public levels:

  • federal
  • state
  • regional
  • municipal
  • private
