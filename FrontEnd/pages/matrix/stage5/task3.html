III.5 Building Operations Market Studies and Marketing Strategies


Market Studies & Marketing Strategy – Building Operations (III.5.)

Determine the right tenant mix.

  • Short term leases vs long term leases.
    • Usually a balance of lease termination dates is important t manage the re-lease risk.
    • A ten-year lease with 2 years left is a short term lease.
  • Big space users vs smaller space users.
    • This depends on the configuration of the property.
    • What is the optimum size for a tenant?
    • Are full-floor users the most efficient space.
  • Office vs. retail vs. residential
    • This is the mixed-use conundrum
    • Each use has different requirements for access, security, and utility use.
    • Ironically, automobile parking is usually the biggest factor in managing these uses.

Determine the existing tenants’ plans for expansion.

  • Existing tenants are the best prospects for leasing vacant space.
  • Building operators need to understand their business plans and future space needs.

Identify tenants who are in the market searching for space.

  • Need to have a working knowledge of the current market activity (even if you are fully leased).
  • Who is moving, how is looking; who is expanding?
  • This part of the “leasing gossip circle” and important market information.

Determine who should approach potential new tenants?

  • Building operators always need to be marketing their space even if it is fully leased.
  • If there is an active re-leasing effort, the Building operator needs to identify a strategy and a communication system to find and attract new tenants.
  • All of the following are involved so it is important who gets paid for what and when:
    • Tenant representatives
    • Landlord representatives
    • Direct (in-house leasing agents)
    • Property managers

Evaluate the pricing of the existing available space.

  • Is the existing space appropriately in terms of competitive space?
  • Are lease terms and concessions appropriate?
